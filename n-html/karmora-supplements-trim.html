Conjugated Linoleic Acid (CLA)

Conjugated linoleic acid (CLA) is the name given to a group of chemicals found in the fatty acid called linoleic acid. According to research, CLA benefits include, helping with weight loss, muscle building and strength improvements, anticancer effects, bone building benefits, growth and development support, reversing atherosclerosis (hardening of the arteries), improving digestion, and reducing food allergies and sensitivities. Major sources of CLA in the diet include full-fat dairy products like beef and butter. Although your immediate reaction may be to link these foods to obesity and heart attacks, they also provide essential CLA, which is a type of polyunsaturated fat. Essential fats we need to obtain from our diet include polyunsaturated omega-3 fatty acids (found in fish, seafood, eggs and in some nuts or seeds) and polyunsaturated omega-6 fats (mostly found in vegetable oils, nuts and seeds). While vastly different, we need both types of these essential fats to balance our immune, hormone, digestive and nervous system functions, which is why low-fat diets may expose certain people to health risks from avoiding enough healthy fats.CLA is best known for blocking weight gain and helping build muscle. The best sources of CLA are found in high-quality beef and butter from healthy, grass-fed cows or other animals. CLA supplements are synthetic forms of the polyunsaturated fat.
